# Step 03: Success Criteria & Product Scope

Define what winning looks like — user success, business success, and scope negotiation.

<critical>Push for SPECIFIC, MEASURABLE outcomes.
NOT "users are happy" → "users complete [action] within [timeframe]".
Scope negotiation: MVP vs. Growth vs. Vision — challenge scope creep.</critical>

---

## User Success

<action>Explore user-centered success:

  **If input docs contain success criteria:** Acknowledge, then refine.
  **If not:** Start with open exploration:
  - When do users feel delighted/relieved/empowered?
  - What's the "aha!" moment?
  - What does "done" look like for the user?

  Make vague metrics specific:
  - "Good adoption" → what percentage by when?
  - "Fast" → how fast, what specifically?
  - "10,000 users" → what kind of users, doing what?</action>

## Business Success

<action>Transition to business perspective:
  - What does 3-month success look like? 12-month?
  - Key metric that says "this is working"?
  - Revenue, user growth, engagement, or other measures?

  Connect success criteria to product differentiator from Step 02.
  Adapt to domain:
  - Consumer: user love, engagement, retention
  - B2B: ROI, efficiency, adoption
  - Regulated: compliance milestones in success criteria</action>

## Scope Negotiation

<action>Guide through three scope levels:
  1. **MVP:** What's essential to prove the concept?
  2. **Growth:** What makes it competitive?
  3. **Vision:** What's the dream version?

  Challenge scope creep: "Could this wait until after launch?"
  For complex domains: ensure compliance minimums in MVP.</action>
